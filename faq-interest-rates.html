Rate Basics & Calculations

What's the difference between interest rate and APR? +

Interest Rate is the percentage charged on your loan principal. It's the basic cost of borrowing.

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the interest rate PLUS all other loan costs like initiation fees, monthly service fees, and credit insurance (if applicable). APR gives you the true annual cost of borrowing.

What's Included in Our APR:
Interest Charges

The cost of borrowing the principal amount

Initiation Fees

One-time fee as per NCA regulations (max R1,150 + VAT)

Monthly Service Fees

Regular administration fees for your loan account

Credit Insurance (Optional)

Protects your payments in case of unforeseen events

Example: A loan with a 15% interest rate might have an 18% APR once all fees are included. Always compare APRs when evaluating loan offers, not just interest rates.

How are interest rates calculated on my loan? +

Our interest rates are calculated based on several transparent factors:

1

Base Rate Determination

We start with the South African prime lending rate as our benchmark, determined by market conditions and SARB policy.

2

Risk Assessment

We evaluate your credit profile, income stability, asset type, and loan-to-value ratio. Better risk profiles receive better rates.

3

Market Positioning

We consider current market rates, economic factors, and competitor offerings to ensure competitive yet sustainable rates.

4

Final Rate Calculation

All factors combine to determine your personalized rate. For variable rates, we establish your spread above prime.

Key Points:

  • Interest is calculated daily but charged monthly
  • Early or extra payments reduce interest from that day forward
  • We provide full rate calculation breakdown before you accept
  • All calculations comply with National Credit Act regulations
What factors determine my specific interest rate? +

Your personalized rate is determined by these key factors:

Primary Rate Determinants:
Credit Profile & History

Your credit score and repayment history (30-40% weighting)

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

Loan amount relative to asset value (20-30% weighting)

Income & Affordability

Stable, verifiable income relative to payments (15-25% weighting)

Asset Type & Quality

Type and condition of collateral asset (10-20% weighting)

Tips to Improve Your Rate:

  • Provide additional collateral to improve LTV ratio
  • Opt for shorter loan terms if affordable
  • Consider a co-signer with strong credit
  • Choose more liquid assets as collateral
  • Maintain stable employment documentation

Rate Types & Comparisons

Should I choose a fixed or variable interest rate? +

The choice depends on your financial situation and risk tolerance:

Fixed vs. Variable Comparison
Feature Fixed Rate Variable Rate
Payment Predictability ✅ Never changes during term ❌ Can increase or decrease
Interest Rate Risk ✅ We bear the risk ❌ You bear the risk
Initial Rate Usually slightly higher Usually starts lower
Rate Changes Locked for entire term Tied to prime rate changes
Best For Budget-conscious borrowers Short-term or flexible borrowers
Early Settlement May have penalties Typically flexible

Our Recommendation:

  • Choose Fixed Rate if: You're on a strict budget, prefer predictable payments, or believe interest rates might rise.
  • Choose Variable Rate if: You can handle payment fluctuations, plan to settle early, or believe rates might fall.
  • Consider a Hybrid: Ask about fixed-for-period options that lock for 1-3 years then convert to variable.

Our consultants can help you choose based on your specific financial situation and market outlook.

How do prime-linked rates work? +

Prime-linked rates are variable rates that move with the South African prime lending rate set by the Reserve Bank.

How Prime-Linked Rates Work:
Your Rate Formula: Prime Rate + Your Personal Spread = Your Interest Rate

Example: If prime rate is 11.75% and your spread is 3%, your interest rate would be 14.75%.

What Affects Prime Rate Changes:
Inflation

SARB adjusts rates to control inflation within 3-6% target

Economic Growth

Rates adjusted to stimulate or cool economic activity

Global Markets

International rate movements influence local decisions

Prime Rate History (Last 3 Years):

  • Current: 11.75% (as of latest SARB announcement)
  • 1 Year Ago: 11.25%
  • 2 Years Ago: 10.50%
  • 3 Years Ago: 9.50%

We notify all clients of prime rate changes within 7 business days and provide revised payment schedules.

Loan Terms & Conditions

How does loan term length affect my interest costs? +

The loan term significantly impacts your total interest paid, even with the same interest rate.

Term Length Impact Example: R200,000 Loan at 15%
Term Length Monthly Payment Total Interest Paid Total Repayment Interest as % of Principal
12 Months R18,026 R16,312 R216,312 8.2%
24 Months R9,698 R32,752 R232,752 16.4%
36 Months R6,933 R49,588 R249,588 24.8%
48 Months R5,567 R67,216 R267,216 33.6%
60 Months R4,758 R85,480 R285,480 42.7%

Strategic Considerations:

  • Shorter Terms (12-24 months): Lower total interest, higher payments, better for income-generating assets
  • Medium Terms (36-48 months): Balance of affordability and total cost, most common choice
  • Longer Terms (60+ months): Lowest payments but highest total interest, best for long-term assets
Important: Always choose the shortest term you can comfortably afford. The difference in total cost between 36 and 60 months on a R200,000 loan is approximately R35,892!
Are there penalties for early settlement? +

We encourage early settlement and our penalty structure is transparent and regulated by the National Credit Act.

1

Early Settlement Calculation

Your early settlement amount includes:

  • Outstanding principal balance
  • Interest up to settlement date
  • Any applicable early termination fee
  • Less unearned interest rebate

2

Interest Rebate

You receive a rebate on future interest using the Rule of 78 method for fixed-rate loans, or simple interest calculation for variable-rate loans.

3

Early Termination Fees

Fixed-rate loans: Maximum 90 days interest as per NCA regulations.
Variable-rate loans: Typically no early termination fees after 6 months.

4

Settlement Certificate

Within 5 business days of full settlement, we provide a paid-up letter and release all collateral documents.

Early Settlement Example:

R100,000 loan at 15% fixed, 36-month term
Settled after 12 months:
• Outstanding balance: R70,420
• Early termination fee: R2,630 (approx. 90 days interest)
• Interest rebate: R3,150
• Total settlement: R69,900
Savings vs. continuing: R14,280 in future interest

We provide a free settlement quote upon request at any time during your loan term.

Multiple Assets
