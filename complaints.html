Complaints Procedure

Last Updated: February 2025

At Lehumo La Edishela, we are committed to providing excellent service to all our clients. However, we understand that sometimes things may not go as expected. If you are dissatisfied with any aspect of our service, we encourage you to let us know so we can address your concerns promptly and fairly.

Our Commitment

We take all complaints seriously and are committed to resolving them in a fair, transparent, and timely manner. Your feedback helps us improve our services.

What Is a Complaint?

A complaint is an expression of dissatisfaction about our products, services, or conduct. This may include, but is not limited to:

  • Service delivery issues
  • Billing or fee disputes
  • Loan agreement concerns
  • Staff conduct
  • Communication problems
  • Processing delays
  • Any other aspect of our service

Information to Include

To help us resolve your complaint quickly, please include:

  • Your full name and contact details
  • Your account or loan reference number (if applicable)
  • Clear description of the issue
  • Any relevant dates
  • What you would like us to do to resolve the issue
  • Copies of any supporting documents

Our Complaint Handling Process

1

Acknowledgment

We will acknowledge receipt of your complaint within 24 hours. You will receive a reference number and the name of the person handling your complaint.

2

Investigation

We will investigate your complaint thoroughly. This may involve reviewing records, speaking with staff, and gathering relevant information. We may contact you for additional details.

3

Resolution Proposal

We will propose a resolution within 15 business days. If we need more time, we will keep you informed of our progress and provide a revised timeline.

4

Implementation

If you accept our proposed resolution, we will implement it promptly. We will confirm in writing when the matter has been resolved.

Complaint Escalation

If you are not satisfied with our proposed resolution, or if we have not resolved your complaint within the agreed timeframe, you may escalate your complaint:

  1. Internal Escalation: Request that your complaint be reviewed by a senior manager.
  2. External Escalation: If still unsatisfied, you may refer your complaint to an external dispute resolution body.

Our Promise to You

  • We will treat you with respect and courtesy throughout the process.
  • We will investigate your complaint thoroughly and impartially.
  • We will keep you informed of progress.
  • We will provide clear reasons for our decisions.
  • We will implement fair resolutions promptly.
  • Your complaint will not affect your ongoing relationship with us.

Important Note

Making a complaint does not affect your legal rights. You have the right to pursue any legal remedies available to you under South African law. We encourage you to contact us first so we have the opportunity to address your concerns.
