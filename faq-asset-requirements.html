Qualifying Assets & Eligibility

What types of assets qualify for loans? +

We accept a wide range of assets as collateral. The key requirements are that the asset has identifiable value, is legally owned by you, and can be properly documented.

Commonly Accepted Asset Categories:
Vehicles & Transport

Cars, trucks, motorcycles, boats, caravans, aircraft (private)

Requirements: Registered, roadworthy, good condition

Property & Real Estate

Residential properties, commercial properties, land, sectional titles

Requirements: Clear title, no major encumbrances

Luxury & High-Value Items

Jewelry, watches, luxury handbags, fine wine, designer items

Requirements: Authenticity proof, good condition

Equipment & Machinery

Construction equipment, farm machinery, manufacturing equipment

Requirements: Working condition, maintenance records

Art & Collectibles

Paintings, sculptures, antiques, rare coins, memorabilia

Requirements: Provenance, authentication, condition

Financial Instruments

Shares, bonds, investment portfolios (in some cases)

Requirements: Liquid, easily valued

Assets We Typically Don't Accept:

  • Items with sentimental value only
  • Perishable goods
  • Illegal or restricted items
  • Assets with unclear ownership
  • Items in very poor condition
  • Extremely specialized equipment with no resale market

Not Sure If Your Asset Qualifies? Contact us with details - we're often able to work with unique or unusual assets if they have clear value and ownership.

What are the minimum requirements for an asset to qualify? +

All assets must meet certain basic criteria to qualify as collateral. Here are our minimum requirements:

1

Clear Legal Ownership

You must be the legal owner with rights to use the asset as collateral. We verify ownership through official documents like title deeds, registration papers, or purchase receipts.

2

Minimum Value Threshold

Assets typically need a minimum value of R50,000, though some specialized assets may have different thresholds. Higher value assets generally get better loan terms.

3

Verifiable Market Value

The asset must have an established market value that can be verified through comparable sales, professional appraisals, or accepted valuation methods.

4

Good Condition & Marketability

The asset should be in reasonable condition for its age/type and have a resale market. We consider factors like age, maintenance, and market demand.

Additional Considerations by Asset Type:

  • Vehicles: Must be roadworthy, registered, and under 15 years old (generally)
  • Property: Must be free of major structural issues and legal disputes
  • Luxury Items: Must have authenticity certificates and be in good condition
  • Equipment: Must be operational with maintenance records
  • Art/Collectibles: Must have provenance and authentication

Exceptions & Special Cases: We sometimes make exceptions for unique situations. If your asset doesn't meet all criteria but has special circumstances, contact us to discuss.

Can I use multiple assets as collateral for one loan? +

Yes, we frequently accept multiple assets as collateral for a single loan. This can be beneficial in several situations:

Benefits of Using Multiple Assets:

  • Higher Loan Amount: Combined value may qualify you for a larger loan
  • Better Loan Terms: Diversified collateral can improve your risk profile
  • Utilize Various Assets: Use different types of assets you own
  • Flexibility: More options if one asset doesn't fully cover your needs
How Multiple Asset Collateral Works:
Combined Valuation

All assets are valued individually, then combined for total collateral value

LTV Calculation

Loan amount based on combined value, with potential for better overall LTV

Single Agreement

One loan agreement covering all assets, simplifying administration

Security Registration

All assets are registered as security for the single loan

Common Multiple Asset Combinations:

  • Vehicle + luxury watch collection
  • Multiple vehicles (personal + business)
  • Property + vehicle
  • Equipment + vehicle
  • Various luxury items (jewelry, watches, handbags)

Process for Multiple Assets:

  1. List all assets you wish to use as collateral
  2. We value each asset individually
  3. Determine combined value and appropriate LTV
  4. Prepare single loan agreement covering all assets
  5. Register security over all assets (where applicable)

Important Considerations:

  • All assets must meet our minimum requirements individually
  • You remain responsible for maintaining all assets
  • Default affects all pledged assets
  • Release of one asset may require loan restructuring

Using multiple assets can be an excellent way to maximize your borrowing power. Contact us to discuss your specific combination of assets.

What documentation do I need for my asset? +

Proper documentation is essential for asset valuation and loan approval. Requirements vary by asset type:

Essential Documentation by Asset Type:
Vehicles
  • Vehicle registration certificate
  • Valid license disc
  • Service history and maintenance records
  • Roadworthy certificate (if applicable)
  • Purchase receipt or previous ownership documents
  • Photos from multiple angles
Property
  • Title deed or ownership certificate
  • Municipal account (not older than 3 months)
  • Rates clearance certificate
  • Latest utility bills
  • Site plans or building plans (if available)
  • Photos of property interior and exterior
Luxury Items
  • Original purchase receipts
  • Certificates of authenticity
  • Brand/manufacturer documentation
  • Appraisal certificates (if previously appraised)
  • Clear photos from all angles
  • Original packaging and accessories (if available)
Additional Documentation:
For All Assets
  • Your ID document (to verify ownership)
  • Proof of address
  • Any financing/loan agreements on the asset
  • Insurance documents (if insured)

If You're Missing Documentation:

  • Contact us first - we may accept alternative documents
  • For vehicles: We can help obtain duplicate registration documents
  • For property: We can assist with obtaining copies of deeds
  • For luxury items: We may accept sworn affidavits or other proof
  • We can often start the process while you gather missing documents

Documentation Tips:

  • Take clear, well-lit photos from multiple angles
  • Keep digital copies of all documents
  • Organize documents by category before submission
  • Note any unique features or recent improvements
  • Update service records/maintenance logs before applying

Verification Process:

  • We verify all documents for authenticity
  • We check for liens or encumbrances on the asset
  • We confirm ownership matches your identity
  • We may request additional documents if needed

Don't let missing documents delay your application - contact us to discuss what alternatives we can accept.

How long does the asset valuation process take? +

Valuation times vary by asset type and complexity. Here's what you can expect:

Standard Valuation Timelines:

  • Vehicles: 24-48 hours (often same day for common models)
  • Property: 3-5 business days
  • Luxury Items: 2-3 business days
  • Equipment: 2-4 business days
  • Art/Collectibles: 3-7 business days (may require specialist)
Factors Affecting Valuation Time:
Asset Complexity

Simple, common assets value faster than unique or specialized items

Documentation Completeness

Complete documentation speeds up the process significantly

Location

Assets in major centers value faster than remote locations

Valuer Availability

Specialist valuers may have scheduling constraints

Expedited Valuation Options:

  • Priority Service: Available for urgent needs (extra fee may apply)
  • Remote Valuation: For some assets, based on photos and documentation
  • Weekend Appointments: Available for urgent cases
  • After-Hours: Possible for business-critical valuations

Valuation Process Steps & Timing:

  1. Initial Review (1-4 hours): We assess provided information and documents
  2. Inspection Scheduling (4-24 hours): We arrange inspection time
  3. Physical Inspection (1-3 hours): Actual inspection time varies by asset
  4. Market Research (4-24 hours): Research comparable sales and values
  5. Report Preparation (4-24 hours): Compiling findings into valuation report
  6. Final Review (2-4 hours): Quality check and approval

How to Speed Up Your Valuation:

  • Provide complete, clear documentation upfront
  • Submit high-quality photos from all angles
  • Be flexible with inspection scheduling
  • Have the asset clean and accessible for inspection
  • Provide comparable sales data if available
  • Respond quickly to any information requests

Updates During the Process:

  • We'll confirm receipt of your application
  • We'll schedule inspection at mutually convenient time
  • We'll provide updates if there are delays
  • We'll share the valuation report once complete

For time-sensitive needs, let us know upfront so we can prioritize your valuation accordingly.

    What happens if my asset gets damaged or decreases in value? +

    We have procedures to handle changes in asset value or condition. Our priority is finding fair solutions:

    If Your Asset Gets Damaged:

    • Report Immediately: Notify us as soon as damage occurs
    • Insurance Claims: File insurance claim if applicable
    • Repair Options: We may require repairs or accept reduced value
    • Revaluation: Asset will be revalued after damage/repair
    • Loan Review: Loan terms may need adjustment based on new value

    If Asset Value Decreases (Market Changes):

    • Normal Fluctuations: Minor value changes typically don't require action
    • Significant Drops: If value drops substantially, we'll contact you
    • Options Available: Several solutions depending on situation
    • Proactive Approach: We monitor markets and may suggest preemptive actions
    Solutions for Decreased Asset Value:
    Partial Loan Repayment

    Reduce loan balance to maintain appropriate LTV ratio

    Additional Collateral

    Provide additional assets to secure the loan

    Loan Restructuring

    Adjust loan terms to reflect current situation

    Refinancing

    Refinance the loan based on current asset value

    Prevention & Protection:

    • Maintain Insurance: Adequate insurance is required
    • Regular Maintenance: Keep assets in good condition
    • Market Awareness: Be aware of factors affecting your asset's value
    • Conservative LTV: Initial conservative LTV provides buffer
    • Periodic Reviews: Regular value reviews catch issues early

    Our Approach:

    • Communication First: We discuss issues before taking action
    • Reasonable Timeframes: We provide time to address issues
    • Multiple Options: We offer various solutions
    • Fair Valuations: We use current market values, not panic prices
    • Preservation Focus: We prefer preserving the arrangement

    When Immediate Action Is Required:

    • Asset becomes uninsured
    • Significant damage without repair plans
    • Asset used illegally or recklessly
    • Attempt to sell or transfer without notification
    • Substantial value drop with no corrective action

    If You're Concerned About Value:

    • Contact us proactively if you anticipate value issues
    • We can arrange early revaluation
    • Discuss preventive measures
    • Explore restructuring options before issues arise

    We understand that asset values can change. Our goal is to work with you to find mutually agreeable solutions when changes occur.

    Is insurance required for assets used as collateral? +

    Yes, insurance is generally required for assets used as collateral. Here are our insurance requirements:

    Mandatory Insurance Requirements:

    • Comprehensive Coverage: Must cover full replacement value
    • Named Insured: You must be the policyholder
    • Loss Payee: We must be listed as loss payee
    • Valid for Loan Term: Coverage must match loan duration
    • Proof of Insurance: Must provide certificate annually
    Insurance Requirements by Asset Type:
    Vehicles
    • Comprehensive motor insurance
    • Theft, fire, accident coverage
    • Third party liability (minimum statutory)
    • Agreed value or market value
    • Roadside assistance (recommended)
    Property
    • Building insurance (full replacement cost)
    • Fire, storm, flood coverage
    • Public liability (if rented out)
    • Contents insurance (if contents included)
    • Rental loss coverage (for investment properties)
    Luxury Items
    • All-risk insurance (jewelry/watches)
    • Worldwide coverage (if traveling with items)
    • Agreed value based on appraisal
    • Theft, loss, damage coverage
    • Mysterious disappearance coverage

    If You Already Have Insurance:

    • We can usually work with your existing policy
    • You may need to add us as loss payee
    • We'll review coverage to ensure it meets requirements
    • You may need to increase coverage amounts
    • No need to cancel and restart insurance

    If You Need Insurance:

    • We can recommend reputable insurers
    • We can help you get appropriate coverage
    • We understand different assets need different policies
    • We can advise on adequate coverage levels

    Insurance Cost Considerations:

    • Insurance cost is your responsibility
    • Consider insurance cost when budgeting for loan
    • Shop around for competitive rates
    • Higher excess can reduce premiums but increases your risk
    • Some assets have specialized insurance markets

    What Happens Without Insurance:

    • Breach of loan agreement terms
    • We may place insurance and charge you
    • Loan could be called for immediate repayment
    • Additional fees and charges may apply
    • Legal action to protect our security interest

    Managing Your Insurance:

    • Keep policy documents accessible
    • Notify us of any policy changes
    • Renew policies before expiration
    • Update coverage if asset value changes significantly
    • Provide renewal certificates annually

    Exceptions & Special Cases:

    • Some low-value assets may not require insurance
    • Business assets may have different requirements
    • Specialized insurance for unique assets
    • Group policies for multiple assets

    Insurance protects both you and us. If you have questions about insurance requirements for your specific asset, contact us for guidance.
