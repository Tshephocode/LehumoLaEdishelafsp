Rate Basics & Calculations

What's the difference between interest rate and APR? +

Interest Rate is the percentage charged on your loan principal. It's the cost of borrowing the money itself.

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the interest rate PLUS all other loan costs like initiation fees, monthly service fees, and any other charges. APR gives you the true cost of borrowing.

What's Included in APR:
Interest Charges

The basic cost of borrowing the principal amount

Initiation Fees

One-time fee to set up the loan (as per NCA regulations)

Monthly Service Fees

Regular fees for administering your loan account

Credit Insurance (if applicable)

Optional insurance to cover loan payments in case of unforeseen events

Example: A loan with a 15% interest rate might have an 18% APR once all fees are included. Always compare APRs when evaluating loan offers, not just interest rates.

Why APR Matters: It allows you to compare different loan offers on a like-for-like basis, showing you the true annual cost of each option.

How are interest rates calculated on my loan? +

Our interest rates are calculated based on several factors, with transparency at every step:

1

Base Rate Determination

We start with the South African prime lending rate as our benchmark. This is determined by the South African Reserve Bank and represents the lowest rate at which banks lend to their most creditworthy customers.

2

Risk Assessment

We evaluate your specific risk profile based on credit history, income stability, asset type, and loan-to-value ratio. Lower risk profiles receive better rates.

3

Market Positioning

We consider current market conditions, competitor rates, and economic factors to ensure our rates remain competitive while sustainable.

4

Final Rate Calculation

All factors are combined to determine your personalized rate. For variable rates, we establish the spread above prime. For fixed rates, we lock in the rate for your chosen term.

Daily vs. Monthly Interest: Most asset-backed loans calculate interest daily but charge it monthly. This means if you make early or extra payments, you save on interest from that day forward.

Transparency: We provide a full breakdown of how your rate was calculated and explain each component before you accept the loan.

What factors determine my specific interest rate? +

Your personalized interest rate is determined by a combination of personal factors and loan characteristics:

Primary Rate Determinants:
Credit Profile & History

Your credit score and repayment history (30-40% weighting). Better credit = better rates.

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

Loan amount relative to asset value (20-30% weighting). Lower LTV = lower risk = better rates.

Income & Affordability

Stable, verifiable income relative to loan payments (15-25% weighting).

Asset Type & Quality

Type of asset securing the loan and its condition/liquidity (10-20% weighting).

Secondary Factors:
Loan Term

Shorter terms often have slightly better rates due to reduced risk exposure.

Market Conditions

Current economic climate, prime rate, and market competition.

Relationship Factors

Existing customer status and banking relationship with us.

Rate Improvement Tips:

  • Provide additional collateral or a larger deposit to improve LTV ratio
  • Opt for a shorter loan term if affordable
  • Consider a co-signer with strong credit if your credit needs improvement
  • Choose more liquid assets as collateral (vehicles over specialized equipment)
  • Maintain stable employment and income documentation

We're transparent about how each factor affects your rate and can advise on ways to improve your rate before applying.

Rate Types & Comparisons

Should I choose a fixed or variable interest rate? +

The choice between fixed and variable rates depends on your financial situation, risk tolerance, and market outlook:

Fixed vs. Variable Rate Comparison
Consideration Fixed Rate Variable Rate
Payment Predictability ✅ Payments never change during fixed term ❌ Payments can increase or decrease
Interest Rate Risk ✅ Protected from rate increases ❌ Exposed to rate increases
Benefit from Rate Drops ❌ Won't benefit if rates fall ✅ Payments decrease if rates fall
Early Settlement Flexibility ❌ May have breakage costs ✅ Usually no penalties for early settlement
Typical Initial Rate Usually higher than variable initially Usually lower than fixed initially
Best For Budget-conscious borrowers, risk-averse individuals, when rates are expected to rise Flexible borrowers, those expecting to pay off early, when rates are expected to fall

Current Market Considerations:

  • When rates are low: Fixed rates lock in the low rate for your term
  • When rates are high: Variable rates may be better if expecting decreases
  • Economic uncertainty: Fixed rates provide stability
  • Short-term needs: Variable rates often better for loans under 3 years

Hybrid Option: Consider a fixed rate for 1-3 years then switching to variable, giving you initial stability with long-term flexibility.

We recommend discussing your specific situation with our rates specialists who can analyze current market conditions and your financial profile to recommend the best option.

How does the prime rate affect my loan interest? +

The South African prime lending rate is the benchmark for most variable rate loans in the country. Here's how it affects your loan:

What is the Prime Rate?

  • The rate at which banks lend to their most creditworthy customers
  • Set by individual banks but typically follows the South African Reserve Bank's repo rate
  • Currently fluctuates based on economic conditions and monetary policy
  • The benchmark for most variable rate loans including home loans, vehicle finance, and credit cards

How It Works with Your Loan:

  • Variable Rate Loans: Your rate = Prime Rate + your personal margin (e.g., Prime + 3%)
  • When Prime Changes: Your rate changes by the same amount (e.g., if Prime increases by 0.5%, your rate increases by 0.5%)
  • Timing: Rate changes typically take effect from your next payment cycle after the Prime change
  • Notification: We notify you of any rate changes affecting your payments

Example Calculation:

  • Prime Rate: 11.75%
  • Your margin: +3.5%
  • Your rate: 15.25% (11.75% + 3.5%)
  • If Prime increases to 12.25%, your new rate becomes 15.75%
  • On a R300,000 loan over 60 months, this 0.5% increase adds approximately R90 to monthly payments

Factors That Influence Prime Rate Changes:

  • South African Reserve Bank repo rate decisions
  • Inflation levels and forecasts
  • Economic growth indicators
  • Currency stability
  • Global economic conditions

Protection Strategies:

  • Fixed Rates: Lock in your rate regardless of Prime movements
  • Rate Cap: Some variable products offer caps limiting how much your rate can increase
  • Overpayment: Pay more when rates are low to reduce principal faster
  • Regular Review: Monitor rates and consider refinancing if advantageous

We monitor Prime Rate movements closely and can advise on strategies to manage rate risk based on your specific loan and financial situation.

Are there penalties for paying off my loan early? +

Our early settlement policy is designed to be fair and transparent:

Variable Rate Loans:

  • No early settlement penalties for variable rate loans
  • You only pay interest up to the settlement date
  • Any unused portion of monthly fees may be refunded proportionally
  • We calculate the exact settlement amount based on the current outstanding balance

Fixed Rate Loans:

  • May have breakage costs if settled during the fixed rate period
  • Breakage costs compensate for interest we would have earned had the loan continued
  • Calculation based on current interest rates vs. your fixed rate
  • Higher costs if current rates are lower than your fixed rate
  • No breakage costs if settled after the fixed rate period ends

Early Settlement Process:

  1. Request Settlement Quote: Contact us for an exact settlement amount
  2. Quote Validity: Settlement quotes are valid for 7-14 days
  3. Payment: Pay the quoted amount by the validity date
  4. Confirmation: Receive written confirmation of loan settlement
  5. Asset Release: Any asset security is released (title deeds returned, etc.)

Benefits of Early Settlement:

  • Save on future interest payments
  • Free up monthly cash flow
  • Remove debt from your credit profile
  • Release collateral for other uses

Partial Early Payments:

  • We welcome partial early payments on most loans
  • Reduces your outstanding principal immediately
  • Lowers total interest paid over loan term
  • Can either reduce monthly payments or shorten loan term (your choice)
  • No fees for partial early payments

Before You Settle Early:

  • Get a written settlement quote showing all amounts
  • Compare cost of early settlement vs. continuing payments
  • Consider any tax implications (for business loans)
  • Ensure you have alternative uses for the funds if needed

We believe in giving our clients flexibility. Contact us to discuss your specific early settlement options and get an exact quote with no obligation.

Rate Management & Changes

Can I switch from variable to fixed rate (or vice versa)? +

Yes, we offer flexibility to switch between rate types in most cases. Here's how it works:

Switching from Variable to Fixed:

  • Available: Usually available at any time
  • Process: Simple application, no full re-application needed
  • New Rate: Based on current fixed rates for your remaining term
  • Benefits: Lock in certainty, protect against rate increases
  • Considerations: May have slightly higher rate than current variable rate
  • Fees: Minimal administration fee may apply (typically R500-R1,500)

Switching from Fixed to Variable:

  • Timing: Usually only at end of fixed rate period
  • During Fixed Term: May have breakage costs similar to early settlement
  • Process: Automatic review at end of fixed period, opt for variable then
  • Benefits: Potentially lower rate if variable rates drop
  • Considerations: Exposure to future rate increases

Hybrid Options:

  • Fixed then Variable: Fix for 1-3 years, then switch to variable
  • Capped Variable: Variable rate with a maximum cap
  • Collared Variable: Variable rate with both minimum and maximum limits

When to Consider Switching:

Variable to Fixed

When rates are low and expected to rise • When you need payment certainty for budgeting • When entering retirement or fixed income situation

Fixed to Variable

When fixed term is ending and variable rates are attractive • When planning to sell asset soon • When expecting significant rate decreases

Switching Process:

  1. Consultation: Discuss your goals with our rates specialist
  2. Analysis: We analyze current rates vs. your situation
  3. Quote: Provide new rate quote and any costs
  4. Decision: You decide if switching makes financial sense
  5. Documentation: Sign simple amendment documents
  6. Implementation: New rate takes effect from next payment cycle

Things to Consider:

  • Compare total cost of remaining loan under both scenarios
  • Consider your risk tolerance and need for certainty
  • Factor in any switching costs or breakage fees
  • Think about your future plans for the asset
  • Consider economic forecasts and rate trends

We recommend a rate review at least annually to ensure you have the best rate structure for your current situation. Contact us for a free rate review and switching analysis.

How often can my variable interest rate change? +

Variable interest rates can change, but there are specific rules and limitations:

Trigger for Rate Changes:

  • Prime Rate Changes: When the South African prime lending rate changes
  • Notice Period: We provide at least 20 business days' notice before rate increases take effect
  • Frequency: Rates can change each time Prime changes, but typically not more than monthly
  • Decreases: Rate decreases typically take effect immediately or from next payment

Typical Change Scenarios:

  • SARB Repo Rate Changes: Usually 6-8 times per year when SARB Monetary Policy Committee meets
  • Bank-Specific Changes: Occasionally banks adjust Prime independently of SARB
  • Economic Events: Significant economic events may trigger rate changes

Change Limits & Protections:

  • No Unlimited Increases: Rates cannot increase without limit in short periods
  • Regulatory Oversight: National Credit Act protects consumers from unfair rate changes
  • Contractual Limits: Some products have maximum increase limits per year

Historical Change Frequency:

  • 2020-2021: Multiple decreases during COVID pandemic
  • 2022-2023: Several increases to combat inflation
  • Typical Year: 2-4 rate changes in a normal economic year
  • Average Change: Usually 0.25% or 0.50% increments

How We Communicate Changes:

  • Written Notice: Letter or email detailing change and effective date
  • SMS Alert: Brief notification of rate change
  • Website Updates: Current rates published on our website
  • Payment Schedule: Updated payment schedule showing new amounts
  • Customer Portal: Real-time updates in your online account

Planning for Rate Changes:

  • Budget Buffer: Consider potential increases when budgeting
  • Rate Alerts: Set up alerts for SARB announcements
  • Regular Reviews: Review your rate structure annually
  • Emergency Fund: Maintain buffer for potential payment increases

If You're Concerned About Increases:

  • Consider switching to fixed rate for certainty
  • Make extra payments when rates are low to reduce principal
  • Discuss rate cap options with our specialists
  • Consider shorter loan term to reduce interest rate risk exposure

We're committed to transparent communication about rate changes. If you have concerns about potential rate increases, contact us to discuss options for managing your interest rate risk.
